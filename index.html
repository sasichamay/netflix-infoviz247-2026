The 2015 Flood: Quantity vs. Quality

Netflix’s global expansion led to a massive spike in content, but more doesn’t always mean better.

2015
Content Volume Trend
Average Quality Trend

Drag across charts or slider to explore

As Netflix aggressively expanded its library to meet global demand, the production volume skyrocketed starting in 2015. While this ‘flood’ of content gave subscribers more choice than ever, the Median IMDb Score began a steady decline. The data reveals a clear trade-off: in the race for global dominance, maintaining consistent quality across thousands of new titles became the ultimate challenge.

Insight

While US volume is huge, its market share is slowly shrinking as other countries start to produce more.

DEEP DIVE

The Global Shift in Content Production

At first glance, Movies still dominate the total library volume. However, a closer look at the growth patterns reveals a significant divergence. While Movies maintain a steady presence, TV Shows are experiencing a localized surge that is reshaping Netflix’s global strategy.

This isn’t just a domestic trend. In the sections following, we will zoom into key markets like the UK, France, and South Korea, where the production of episodic content is not just growing—it’s gaining massive momentum, redefining what ‘Global Content’ truly means.

GENRE DOMINANCE

Netflix wants you to watch Drama

Across all 18 genre categories on Netflix, Drama appears in nearly 1 of every 2 titles
